# AI Buddy as a Catalyst for UZH's Pedagogical Transformation

> Created: 9 April 2026
> Context: Year Review preparation (21 April 2026), strategic direction for Steering Board

---

## 1. The Gap: Students Use AI, UZH Doesn't Guide Them

DSI survey (March 2024, n=900+):
- **97%** of UZH students use generative AI tools
- **90%** have used ChatGPT in the last 6 months
- **67%** would use an "AI Buddy" as a study companion
- Only **14%** learned to use AI through teaching staff
- Only **6%** through UZH courses

International benchmarks confirm the trend:
- **92%** of UK students use AI tools (HEPI/Kortext, Dec 2024)
- **86%** globally (Digital Education Council, 15 countries)

**The paradox is stark**: the university trains students to think critically, yet 94% of them learn to use the most transformative tool of their generation outside any institutional framework.

---

## 2. The UZH Mission (Leitbild) as a Compass

The UZH mission statement (Leitbild, 2012) explicitly prescribes:

> *"Teaching at UZH is research-based [...] develops students' ability to formulate and answer questions, to methodically gain insight into an issue, to critically assess problems and to share knowledge."*

Generative AI touches **every single one** of these four pillars:

1. **Formulating questions**: AI fundamentally changes how questions are asked (prompting as a skill)
2. **Methodical insight**: research workflows now integrate AI at every stage
3. **Critical assessment**: evaluating AI outputs IS the critical competency of the 21st century
4. **Sharing knowledge**: AI transforms how knowledge is created and disseminated

Failing to integrate AI into teaching means **contradicting UZH's own mandate**.

---

## 3. Why AI Buddy Hasn't Found Product-Market Fit (Yet)

AI Buddy currently covers **Family 1** (Information & Search): courses, regulations, UZH services. This is useful, but:

- Students can find this information (painfully) on their own
- It doesn't create a daily habit
- **50% of queries in v1.0 were course content questions** (out of scope)
- D+30 retention remains a challenge

The most desired, most natural, most obvious use case, the one students already practice MASSIVELY with ChatGPT, is: **using AI to learn**. And that is exactly the one AI Buddy excluded (Family 4, January 2026 decision).

---

## 4. The Dilemma: Why It's Complicated

Touching teaching means touching the core of the university's mission. It involves:

| Dimension | Complexity | Stakeholders |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Pedagogical** | AI must reinforce learning, not replace it | VP EDU, education sciences, lecturers |
| **Academic** | Each course has its own learning objectives | Lecturers, Studiendekanate |
| **Ethical** | Risk of dependency, plagiarism, atrophy of critical thinking | Ethics commission, Rechtsstelle |
| **Quality** | An AI tutor giving wrong answers is worse than no tutor | QA, lecturers |
| **Political** | Some lecturers see AI as a threat | Faculty associations |
| **Legal** | Intellectual property of course materials | Rechtsstelle, lecturers |

This complexity is precisely what led to the exclusion of Family 4.

---

## 5. The Strategic Pivot: AI Buddy as a Transformation Platform

The idea is **not** for AI Buddy to become an AI tutor overnight. The idea is for AI Buddy to become the **institutional vehicle** through which UZH progressively integrates generative AI into its teaching services.

### Phase 1: AI Literacy Companion (now, H2 2026)

**What we already have, repositioned differently.**

- AI Buddy helps students **understand how to use AI** in their academic journey
- Guides students toward the right UZH tools (KlickerUZH, EducationAI, OLAT)
- Provides information on UZH AI policies (what is allowed, what is not)
- Integrates the UNESCO AI Competency Framework (4 competencies: human-centred mindset, ethics, techniques, system design)
- **No course content, but a meta-competency: knowing how to use AI to learn**

### Phase 2: OLAT Opt-in Tutoring (H1 2027)

**The Steering Board already suggested this (10 March 2026).**

- Lecturers voluntarily enable AI tutoring for their course via OLAT
- AI Buddy accesses course materials (slides, scripts, exercises) only if the lecturer authorizes it
- The lecturer retains full control: scope, limits, disclaimers
- KlickerUZH and Ethel (ETH) already validate this model (15K+ queries on KlickerUZH)

### Phase 3: Institutional Learning Analytics (2027+)

**AI Buddy as a sensor of the transformation.**

- Aggregated, anonymized data on student learning needs
- Input for Studiendekanate: which modules cause problems? which regulations are misunderstood?
- Feedback loop to lecturers: students ask X questions about a specific concept
- Alignment with the AI Competence Hub's AI Hexagon (6 axes of AI integration in curricula)

---

## 7. The Argument for the Steering Board (21 April)

UZH has **two options**:

**Option A (status quo)**: AI Buddy remains an administrative navigation tool. Useful, but niche. Students continue using ChatGPT to learn, outside any institutional framework. UZH misses the pedagogical transformation train.

**Option B (pivot)**: AI Buddy becomes the **institutional platform** through which UZH progressively, in a controlled and ethical manner, integrates generative AI into its teaching services. Not a replacement for lecturers, but a **catalyst** that helps the university adapt its processes.

Option B is aligned with:
- The UZH Leitbild (2012)
- The DSI Roadmap 2026/27
- The AI Competence Hub's AI Hexagon
- The UNESCO AI Competency Framework for Students (2024)
- The OECD "Empowering Learners for the Age of AI" framework (2025)
- The Swiss federal ERI strategy 2025-2028

It addresses students' #1 need. It gives UZH leadership in the Swiss EdTech landscape, ahead of ETH (Ethel) and other Swiss universities.

## 9. Risks of This Pivot

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Lecturer resistance | Strict opt-in, lecturer retains full control |
| Insufficient tutoring quality | DeepEval, per-course ground truth, Langfuse monitoring |
| Scope creep | Strict phasing, each phase validated before the next |
| Internal competition (KlickerUZH, EducationAI) | Complementary positioning, not competing |
| LLM provider dependency | Modular architecture, LiteLLM abstraction |
